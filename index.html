About me

Astrophysics and Programming

I recently completed my Ph.D. at the Université de Montréal studying Extra-Galactic Astrophysics. On this site you will be able to find my current and past projects as well as relevant information about me! My wife, Catherine, and I live in the old port of Montréal with our two adorable cats (plenty of photos to come!). We love skating and strolling around historic Montréal. In addition to studying Astronomy at UdeM (l'Université de Montréal), I study French, Spanish, and Russian.

After my first stint as a graduate student at Duke University in computational mechanics and scientific computing, I returned to astrophysics by joining Dr. Julie Hlavacek-Larrondo’s high-energy astrophysics team at l’Université de Montréal. Immersing myself in the astronomy life in Montreal, I developed machine learning tools for spectral analysis and worked on understanding the dynamics of distant galaxy clusters. I now work as an Algorithm & Software Developer at Dragonfly FRO, where I build advanced image processing algorithms and software for revolutionary distributed aperture telescopes, pushing the boundaries of ultra-low surface brightness astronomy.
